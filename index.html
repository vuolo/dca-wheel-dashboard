What the numbers mean

DCAP

Durable Competitive Advantage Points — the canonical 11-parameter scorer (Jimenez & Vuolo Technical Plan) over 4 fiscal years of filings: inventory & earnings trend, earning power, PP&E, return on assets, debt load, adjusted debt-to-equity, preferred stock, retained-earnings growth, buybacks, return on equity, goodwill. 17 points max (15 when equity history excuses one test).

Gift

A name scoring ≥60% of its applicable DCAP max. The thesis: sell puts only where being forced to buy 100 shares at the strike is a purchase we'd celebrate — then assignment is not a loss.

Cash-secured put (CSP)

Sell a put and hold the full strike×100 in cash. Outcomes: the put expires worthless (keep the premium) or we're assigned the shares at an effective discount (strike − premium) and the wheel continues with covered calls.

Efficiency

(premium ÷ strike×100) ÷ days to expiration — daily yield per dollar of capital at risk, shown annualized (×365). Normalizing by capital AND time makes every candidate comparable; faster capital recycling compounds harder than bigger single premiums.

Delta (Δ)

The put's price sensitivity; ~rough odds of finishing in the money. We cap |Δ| at 0.30 so we're paid to wait, not coin-flipping into assignment.

IV Rank (IVR*)

Where today's volatility sits vs the stock's own trailing year, 0–100. Higher = richer premium for the same risk. *v1 uses a realized-volatility rank proxy — the broker exposes no implied-volatility history.

Premium yield

Premium ÷ (strike × 100). The screen requires >1.5% so the trade pays enough to be worth securing the capital.

Fails

Which screen rules that specific play violates, with the actual number vs the threshold. Dimmed rows are kept deliberately — near-misses show where each ladder is richest.

The 50% exit

Stage 4: buy back any short put at 50% of max profit — the first half of the premium decays fastest with the least risk. Secondary exits: underlying −8% vs entry, or 21 DTE remaining. Never hold to expiration chasing the last drop.
